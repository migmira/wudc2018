WUDC 2018

Masters Tournament

Spanish is one of the most important languages in the world due to the amount of people that speak the language, especially in areas such as Latin America and Spain. Spanish is spoken in almost all of Latin America, a few exceptions being Brazil and some French and Dutch domains in the Caribbean. Spanish is the second most spoken language by native speakers, and the third most spoken language worldwide. Taking the previous information into account, it is no surprise that the Spanish-speaking debate community is also of a considerable size, and has been growing constantly over the past years. It is also worth mentioning, that Spanish is the only other language in which a world universities debating championship is held: the Campeonato Mundial de Debate Universitario en Español (CMUDE). Although the official language of WUDC México 2018 is English, the Organisation Committee also seeks to foster the culture of debate in Mexico and has, therefore, decided to accordingly host the Masters tournament in Spanish. In implementing Spanish as the official language of the Masters tournament for WUDC México 2018, we aim to offer a space for the Spanish-speaking debate community and to achieve a greater audience and diffusion of the tournament at both a national level and in other Spanish-speaking countries.
