David Alatorre López

David started competing in debate in high school, when he was selected to be part of Team México's World Schools Debating Championship (WSDC) team for Qatar 2010. When he realized that some of his competitors from around the world had been to dozens of competitions before and had college debate circuits, he decided he had a moral responsibility to help debate grow in his country. This meant that he became the first Mexican in many things —almost always alongside another Mexican: first Mexican to compete at a BP tournament (2010); to found a BP debate team; to reach a grand final abroad (2010) and in Europe, the United States, and South America; to compete at a world championship; to reach the grand final of the Spanish Worlds (Campeonato Mundial de Debate Universitiario en Español [CMUDE]) in 2012; to receive a speaker award at both Spanish and English Worlds (2nd in CMUDE 2011 and 5th best EFL speaker at WUDC 2013); to be part of the adjudication core of an international tournament (DCA at CMUDE 2014); to convene and be CA of a tournament in Mexico; to be CA at an international tournament (Pan-Ams 2016), and to be an independent, subsidized adjudicator at WUDC 2016.

David has an unbridled love for his country and has found in debate a way to help make it a better place and that is why he —along with people he calls friends— founded the Asociación Mexicana de Debate A.C. [Mexican Debate Association], of which he is the first president and which has successfully bid to bring the 38th WUDC for the first time not only to Latin America and the Caribbean but to the entire Spanish-speaking world.

Arturo Cuevas Bautista

He studied Political Science and Law at the Instituto Tecnológico Autónomo de México (ITAM). In 2012, he was recognized as the Best Debater at the National Congress of Debate. Later that year, he represented his university in WUDC Berlin 2013. That same year, he co-organized the first independent Presidential Debate. In 2014, he collaborated as an advisor in the ITAM’s student union. He co-created Universus, a debate tv show and tournament which aired in UNOTV for two seasons.

In 2015, he was an independent candidate to Mexico City’s Congress. He has collaborated in different media as a political commentator. During 2016, he was a parliamentary advisor to the President of Mexico City’s Congress, and he was also an advisor to one of the mayors of the sixteen administrative division in which Mexico City is divided. Currently, he is a legal and political advisor, and Deputy Convenor of WUDC México 2018.

Monserrat Legorreta

Montserrat began her debating career in high school by winning best speaker at the only debating competition in México at the time. She became president and founder of the High School Debate organization at the ITESM CSF and led the first large scale debate tournament successfully, which allowed for the creation of the high school debate league. Legorreta is majoring in International Relations at the ITESM CSF, is the founder of the debate club and has organized large scale events, leading diverse teams and bringing complex ideas to life.

She was part of the Mexican Team during México’s Presidency of the G20 Young Entrepreneurs’ Alliance working on the Steering Committee and governance. In 2013 she was one of eight students to receive a scholarship to attend Yale University as part of the YVISP program and in 2014 she won the Yale Debate Adams Cup. She worked for FEMSA as an institutional relations analyst and is currently the VicePresident of the Asociación Mexicana de Devate. She is a former National Mexican Debate Champion and has participated in WUDC in 2013 and 2016.

Marco Vinicio Bárcena Rodríguez

He studied International Relations at the Instituto Tecnológico Autónomo de México (ITAM), in which he founded two student organisations: Debate ITAM and TEDxITAM. In addition, he was an advisor in the ITAM’s student union. Marco has been a part of the world of debate since 2007, year in which he was selected to form a part of the team that would represent Mexico at the World Schools Debating Championship (WSDC), an achievement he would repeat in 2008. He would later come to be team manager and junior coach for Team Mexico WSDC. He also participated in WSDC 2009, 2011 and 2012 as a judge. In 2012, he joined the group of panelists for the political commentary programme Sin Filtro on ForoTV.

Rodrigo Ponciano Ojeda

Rodrigo was introduced to the world of debate at an ESU Summer Workshop in 2010. He then went on to become part of Team Mexico WSDC Dundee in 2011, the first time that Mexico broke to elimination rounds. He was the captain and junior coach for the Edron Academy debate team from 2011-2012. As a debater from the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM), he was champion in the 1st ITAM IV BP Tournament in the English division, and an octofinalist in CMUDE (Worlds in Spanish) 2014. Since August 2016 he is the coach of the Bachillerato Alexander Bain, and has been an instructor at the Mexican Summer Debate Camp for six years.

Rodrigo majored in English Literature at UNAM, and at UNAM helped with the organisation of several academic events, experience which now serves him well in his current role.

Rodrigo Flores

Rodrigo Flores volunteered at his university’s writing centre. He also participated in literacy and adult education campaigns sponsored by the Mexican government. He briefly got involved with debate while in high school.

Adolfo Romero

Adolfo studied Biotechnological Engineering at the Tecnológico de Monterrey - Campus Estado de México (Tec CEM). He’s always believed that a good rhetoric and knowledge can take humanity farther than anything else.

In that spirit, he joined the Tec CEM’s debating team in 2013, and at the same time, he cofounded an institutional radio show called Tierra Baldía; a cultural diffusion program where he was a broadcaster and the producer.

In 2014, in collaboration with the School of Economics, he organized the first multidisciplinary debate tournament to take place at Tec CEM, where the team he mentored obtained first place.

Valentina Palma

Valentina Palma Oliveros is in her final year of Social Communication and Journalism at the Universidad de Ibagué. She has been a content producer and media coordinator at the Colombian communications agency, Nephila, and she has also participated in the management of communications in projects related to the inclusion of persons with disabilities. Samantha Mena

Hi! My name is Samantha Mena, debate has been a passion of mine since I was little. The first time that it caught my eye was when I was in 9th grade. But it wasn’t until I was in high school that it stopped being just a simple hobby and turned to be a big part of my world. I was part of the Churchill College Debate team and participated in every single tournament that the Circuit organized almost monthly. I had my peak during 2013, when I was selected as one of the team members that would be representing Mexico in the Panamerican Schools’ Debating Championship. That adventure went on for another year, when once again in 2014 I was chosen to be a part of this team once more.

For reasons I still don’t fully understand, when I had to choose a BA I went for Graphic Design. Regardless of this my debate career wasn’t over as I was Co-coach of the Prepa IBERO debate team until 2016. After that I wanted to expand the Debate culture all through the University and so I went on and founded the debating society at a university level at IBERO, and I am currently president of that same society.

When I heard that WUDC was taking place in Mexico City, I wanted to be part of it any way I could. That is how I got the opportunity of mixing both of my biggest passions and joining the design team. Giving back a little bit to the community that gave me so much. You can see more of my design work here.

Esther Montserrat Pérez Cisneros

A native of Guadalajara, Jalisco, she studies Law at the Tecnológico de Monterrey (Tec) - Campus Guadalajara and a Political Science and Public Administration degree at the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM). She worked at the State of Jalisco’s Commission on Human Rights, where she was a research assistant at the Instituto de Investigación y Capacitación en Derechos Humanos. She won Tec’s Beca de Liderazgo scholarship for her high school studies and the Beca de Especialidades 2013 scholarship for her Law degree.

During her high school studies at Tec, she was elected President of the Alumni Society and was noted for her organisation of various social benefit events. For this reason, she was nominated for Tec’s highest award: Alumno Integral. During her professional training, Montserrat was elected as representative for the University of Guadalajara’s Ignacio L. Vallarta Legislature as a result of her work on a bill about independent party political candidates. In 2015, she was elected as one of Mexico’s four junior representatives at the United Nations’ 70th General Assembly and, later, as a delegate to the World Federation of United Nations Associations.

Laura Alejandra Sánchez

Laura Alejandra Sánchez has been a very active member of numerous Volunteer Departments in the Mexican debating circuit; she started in 2014 with CMUDE Mexico held by the Tecnológico de Monterrey at Campus Estado de México. Later on, she acted as Volunteers Director in the first Mexican Universities Debating Championships in both English and Spanish. With regard to debate, she first started as a debater in the Pan-American Championship held in Jamaica in 2016, reaching semi-finals and was the 11th best speaker of the contest; she also broke at the Campeonato Nacional de Debate Guadalajara 2016 and was the best novice speaker of the championship and, recently, sub-champion of the Mexican Universities Debating Championship Tequila 2016 as well as 4th best speaker.

Nowadays, she is interested in involving the Mexican youth into the world of debate through their participation in small tournaments in the national circuit up to into championships such as WUDC and CMUDE, she aims to aid in empowering them and to increase their political and social participation in order to improve the living conditions of their surroundings. She aims to help do this not only in Mexico, but also in Spain where she is continuing her mayor in International Relations.

Adriana García

She is a founding member of the Debate Club at the Faculty of Engineering, UNAM with other friends from school (yes, engineers like and can debate too!). She was thrilled with the idea of WUDC happening in Mexico, so she decided to be part of the Org Comm, because “people (especially our generation) should be able to be involved in more debating activities, due to all the skills ( mental and emotional) it helps you develop", at least in her experience. She’s also a people person, which helps explain why she’s part of the incredible volunteers team.
