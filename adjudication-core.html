Michael Dunn Goekijan

Michael was crowned Best Speaker in the World at WUDC 2016, where he was also a Grand Finalist, and Best Speaker in Europe at EUDC 2016, where he also won. He was previously the Second Best Speaker at WUDC 2015, where he also topped the team tab. Cumulatively, Michael has won around thirty debating tournaments including the Yale IV, the International Mace and the HWS Round Robin, and has been best speaker of a further thirty or so, including the Oxford IV (twice) and the HWS Round Robin (twice). In addition to his role as Co-CA of WUDC 2018, he was a DCA of WUDC 2017, and has served on at least two-dozen Adjudication Cores at tournaments around the world.

Mifzal Mohammed

Perhaps one of the most successful Asian debaters of all time, Mifzal was Fourth Best Speaker and an Open Semi-Finalist at WUDC 2017, after breaking second for the second time in a row. He is also a former Champion, Best Speaker and Chief Adjudicator of the Asian British Parliamentary Debating Championship (ABP). Mifzal has won well over a dozen debating tournaments, including the Cambridge IV and the Malaysian National Championships (thrice), and appeared in many more finals, including three all-Asian Grand Finals and an Australs Semifinal. He has served on the Adjudication Core of more than 15 tournaments in over 7 countries, serving not only as Co-CA of WUDC MÃ©xico 2018, but also as Co-CA of the United Asians Debating Championship 2017 (UADC). Additionally, Mifzal is deeply involved in the organisation of international debating, and served on the Organising Committee of WUDC 2015 and Australs 2013.

Sella Nevo

Sella is a World ESL champion, a European ESL champion, and a European ESL best speaker. He was DCA of Zagreb Euros, and has CAed the Durham IV, Leiden Open, Vienna IV, Paris Open and over a dozen other tournaments. He has judged the finals of WUDC and EUDC multiple times, and has been the coach of the Tel Aviv Debating Society since 2012.

Wasifa Noshin

Hailing from Bangladesh, Wasifa was part of the Australs Adjudication Core in 2016. She has broken in the Open Category as a debater at Worlds and has ranked in the top teams at multiple Asian tournaments, reaching Semi-Finals and Finals at the Asian British Parliamentary Debating Championship (ABP) in 2014 and 2015, and ranking as the Third Best Speaker at UADC 2015. She was also part of the Adjudication Core at Australasian Womenâs in 2015, Shanghai Debate Open in 2016 and will serve as a DCA of Krabi ABP 2017.

Steven Penner

Steven is a Canadian National Champion in both CP and BP, as well as a BP National Top Speaker. He's also an HWS Round Robin Finalist, and after winning the 2016 Hart House IV, has finally (and gratefully) retired from speaking competitively. As a judge, he has served on the Adjudication Cores of 2 Canadian BP Championships, CUSID CP Nationals, a Hart House IV, 2 NAUDC's, 2 CUSID/APDA NorthAms, and a USUDC, as well as judging an HWS Round Robin Final and 3 WUDC Semi-Finals. He is now the assistant coach at HWS, and along with Eric Barnes, consistently runs major WUDC-prep tournaments ahead of schedule.

Paul Smith

Paul reached the Semi-Finals at WUDC 2016, and the Octo-Finals at WUDC 2015. He has also judged a WUDC Final, and broken as a judge at Australs and EUDC. Paul has twice reached the Semi-Finals of Australs, the Finals of the Sydney Mini and those of the New Zealand BP Championships, winning the latter in 2009. For good measure, he was also a Top 10 Speaker at the North American Debating Championships in 2012.

Evan Lynyak

Evan was an Open Grand Finalist at WUDC 2017, and twice a Top 10 Open Speaker. In addition, she is a North American and Pan-American Champion and Top Speaker, and a US Grand Finalist and top 5 Speaker. Evan was also a Finalist and Top Speaker at the 2016 Oxford IV, and a Semifinalist and Top 10 Speaker at the 2016 Cambridge IV. Evan has CA-ed the 2016 Yale IV and Cambridge Women's Open.
