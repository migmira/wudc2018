Mexico City

Few cities in the world are as fascinating and incredible as Mexico City, capital of Mexico. Located in the central part of the country, Mexico City has a unique history that is reflected in its cultural diversity up to this day. The city was built over the Aztec capital of Tenochtitlan in the early sixteenth century and then served as the centre of the Spanish empire’s colonies in the Americas. After Mexico achieved its independence from Spain in 1821, Mexico City became the capital of the incipient nation.

The richness of the city’s culture can be appreciated in its World Heritage Sites such as its Historic Centre, Xochimilco, Luis Barragan’s House and Studio and the University City of the National Autonomous University of Mexico. Furthermore, Mexico City has the most museums of any city in the world, a testimony to the city’s status as a cultural hub. Among these are Chapultepec Castle, the only royal castle ever to have existed in the Americas and which now serves as the National Museum of History; the world-renown National Museum of Anthropology; and numerous archaeological sites such as the Templo Mayor [Main Temple], the ruins of one of the most important temples in the old Tenochtitlan.

Nevertheless, Mexico City’s culture is not something only witnessed in museums, but also in its day-to-day life. Mexican cuisine is considered an Intangible Cultural Heritage by the UNESCO, and in Mexico City, a cultural crossroads for all the country, one may find countless places in which to try its many dishes and beverages. One may also enjoy the lively colours and the hustle and bustle of distinguished marketplaces in neighbourhoods such as Coyoacan and San Angel. Walking along the streets you may also see and hear another one of Mexico’s Intangible Cultural Heritages: mariachis. The nightlife of the city is also vibrant, and there are plenty of bars and clubs in neighbourhoods like Roma, Condesa and Polanco to cater for entertainment at the end of the day.

In the past and present, Mexico City has thus always distinguished itself for its embracing attitude towards cultural difference; through cultivating debate and hosting WUDC 2018, we hope to not only continue but increase this attitude into the future.
